# content-strategist

**Status:** Not yet implemented — vision document only.
**Category:** Specialist

---

## Purpose

`content-strategist` owns the narrative architecture and content structure of presentations. It is responsible for the story — the argument being made and how it is organized — not the visual design or the implementation.

---

## Responsibilities

### Narrative Flow
- Designing the high-level argument the presentation makes
- Ensuring the opening creates tension and earns attention before providing context
- Ensuring the closing is a conclusion, not a summary
- Validating that each section earns its place in the narrative

### Audience Adaptation
- Tailoring narrative architecture for the primary audience
- Adjusting depth, tone, and framing for the audience's knowledge and concerns
- Deciding what to include and what to exclude for this specific audience
- Identifying what is "too much detail" versus "not enough rigor" for a given audience

### Information Architecture
- Organizing complex information into a clear, logical hierarchy
- Determining what belongs in the main narrative flow versus as supporting or appendix material
- Deciding where to use data, story, analogy, or demonstration as the primary vehicle

### Section Organization
- Defining section boundaries and the purpose of each section
- Writing section opener framing (the question or tension that opens each section)
- Ensuring each section has a clear question, development, and resolution

### Simplifying Complex Ideas
- Translating technical or complex concepts for the specific audience
- Finding the right analogy or example for each complex idea
- Identifying where complexity serves the argument versus where it obscures it

---

## Output Format

`content-strategist` produces in Phase 2 (Architecture):

1. **Story map** — the sequence of arguments the presentation makes (not slides)
2. **Section outline** — major sections with their purpose, opening tension, and key argument
3. **Slide sketches** — rough slide-by-slide content outline (headlines and content types)

In Phase 3 (Content):

4. **Full slide content** — headline (argument), body content, speaker notes for every slide
5. **Content flags** — ideas that need development, data that needs sourcing, claims needing validation

---

## The Three-Layer Standard

Every piece of content must pass the Three-Layer Model check before proceeding:

| Layer | Check |
|---|---|
| **What** | Is the information communicated clearly? |
| **Why** | Is it obvious why this matters to this specific audience? |
| **So what** | Is there a clear thought, feeling, or action the slide produces? |

Content that delivers only Layer 1 is not presentation content — it is reference material.
