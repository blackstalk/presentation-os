# Prototype Objectives

**Prototype version:** v0.1
**PAS version:** 1.0
**Architecture Review:** [Date — must be complete before this document is written]

This document defines what Prototype v0.1 is for. Read this before building. Read this before reviewing.

---

## What We Are Validating

- [ ] Narrative flow — does the story move logically from section to section?
- [ ] Learning Journey — does the emotional arc feel true at each stage?
- [ ] Navigation — can a reviewer move through the deck naturally?
- [ ] Fragment timing — do reveals land at the right moment in the verbal explanation?
- [ ] Component consistency — do reusable components look and behave the same across slides?
- [ ] Diagram readability — are diagrams legible and do they build in the right sequence?
- [ ] Theme direction — does the overall visual tone feel right for this audience?

## What We Are NOT Optimizing in v0.1

- Final graphics and illustration polish
- Pixel-perfect spacing and alignment
- Motion and animation polish
- Accessibility refinements (WCAG AA audit comes in Design Refinement)
- Speaker notes (written in Stage 12, not Stage 7)
- Performance optimization
- Mobile or print layout

## Success Criterion

**Prototype v0.1 succeeds if:**

> "An internal reviewer can experience the presentation from beginning to end without verbal explanation and still understand the story."

If the answer is yes: Prototype succeeded. Proceed to Experience Review.
If the answer is no: identify specifically where the story breaks, revise the PAS, regenerate.

## Prototype Version Naming

| Version | What it is |
|---|---|
| v0.1 | First generated version — validates architecture |
| v0.2+ | Revised versions — address Experience Review findings |
| v1.0 | Production — delivered to audience |

Never call v0.1 "the presentation." It is the prototype.
